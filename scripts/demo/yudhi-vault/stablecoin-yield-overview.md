# Stablecoin Yield Overview

Stablecoin yields come from three buckets:

1. **Lending markets** like Aave/Compound (see [[lending-markets]]) — low risk but rates compress with TVL.
2. **Liquidity-providing on Curve/Uniswap stable pools** (see [[curve-stableswap]]) — yield from swap fees plus token incentives, with impermanent loss risk only in depeg.
3. **RWA exposure** via Maple, Centrifuge, Ondo (see [[rwa-tokenization]]) — credit risk and longer lockups, but 8-12% sustainable.

## Picking between them

The right bucket depends on risk tolerance and capital lockup. Lending sits at the safe end with the lowest yield. Curve LP-ing is the middle case, mostly safe except in depegs. RWAs are the highest yield but introduce credit and regulatory risk that varies by jurisdiction (see [[malaysian-regulatory-context]] for the Malaysian view).
